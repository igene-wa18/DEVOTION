The Nine Divine Forms of Mata

Day 1

Maa Shailputri

– Daughter of the mountains; beginning of life and purity.

She is the source of all divine energy — Shakti in its purest form.

Iconography

Rides a white bull (Nandi). Holds a Trishul 🔱 and lotus. A crescent moon adorns her forehead signifying spiritual balance.

Symbolises

Strength, courage, peace, and protection. She embodies purity, devotion, and the power to overcome adversity.

Day 2

Maa Brahmacharini

– Embodiment of penance, knowledge, and spiritual discipline.

Walking barefoot with a rosary, she shows the path of truth and devotion.

Iconography

Wears a pure white sari. Walks barefoot (humility). Holds a japa mala and kamandal — symbols of discipline.

Symbolises

Devotion, wisdom, patience, emotional balance, and inner strength through spiritual practice.

Day 3

Maa Chandraghanta

– Fierce warrior; crescent moon like a bell; destroyer of evil.

The sound of her bell banishes darkness and fills the world with divine energy.

Iconography

Rides a lion 🦁. Has 10 hands — trident, sword, bow, arrow, lotus. One hand always in Abhaya mudra (blessing).

Symbolises

Bravery, removal of negativity and fear — balance between calmness and power.

Day 4

Maa Kushmanda

– Creator of the universe; cosmic energy and warmth like the sun.

She created the universe with her divine smile — a single beam of cosmic light.

Iconography

Rides a lion 🦁. Has 8 hands — kamandal, bow, arrow, lotus, amrit kalash, chakra, gada, jap mala.

Significance

Represents creation, energy, and vitality. Destroys darkness and fills life with positivity.

Day 5

Maa Skandamata

– Mother of Lord Skanda (Kartikeya); protective motherhood.

Her love for Kartikeya is the purest form of unconditional divine devotion.

Iconography

Rides a lion 🦁. Holds Lord Kartikeya in her lap and a lotus 🌸 in two hands. One hand in blessing posture.

Symbolises

Motherly love, purity, devotion — removal of ignorance and the blessing of wisdom (Moksha).

Day 6

Maa Katyayani

– Warrior goddess; daughter of Sage Katyayana; slayer of demons.

Born from the combined wrath of all gods — the ultimate force of righteousness.

Iconography

Rides a lion 🦁. Four hands — sword, lotus, Abhaya mudra (protection), Varsha mudra (wish-granting).

Symbolises

Courage, destruction of evil, justice, and righteousness. The warrior face of divine feminine energy.

Day 7

Maa Kaalratri

– Fearsome dark form; destroys ignorance and all evil forces.

She is called "Shubhankari" — the fierce one who ultimately brings only good.

Iconography

Rides a donkey 🐴. Dark complexion (infinite cosmos). Holds a sword and iron hook. Hands in Abhaya and Varda mudra.

Symbolises

Destruction of evil, fearlessness, and end of darkness. Despite her fierce form she brings auspiciousness.

Day 8

Maa Mahagauri

– Symbol of purity, peace, and beauty; inner cleansing and calmness.

Her radiance illuminates all the three worlds with divine purity and grace.

Iconography

Rides a white bull 🐂. Holds Trishul 🔱 and Damru. Four hands in Abhaya mudra and Varada mudra.

Symbolises

Purity, simplicity, peace — removal of past sins and spiritual growth with clarity of heart.

Day 9

Maa Siddhidatri

– Bestower of all supernatural powers; the final perfect form of Durga.

She grants the eight siddhis — completing every devotee's spiritual journey.

Iconography

Seated on a lotus 🌸 or rides a lion 🦁. Holds chakra, gada, shankha, and lotus. Calm, divine, and graceful.

Symbolises

Spiritual power, enlightenment, fulfillment, perfection, and divine wisdom. The ultimate blessing of Navratri.
